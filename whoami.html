I created this site primarily to keep a written record of my progress on various challenge sites and projects. However, I believe that sharing is key to progress, and I have often needed to read walkthroughs by other people to learn about their methods and unblock myself. That's why I want to share my limited knowledge with others who, like me, are just starting out and need a little help.

On this site, I will be posting my walkthroughs for challenges on TryHackMe, Root-Me, and CryptoHack. I will also be sharing updates on some of my mini-projects in the Code Snippets section, as they may be of interest to some of you.

Please be indulgent with me, as I am not a web developer. However, I am open to constructive criticism, so feel free to give me feedback if you feel so inclined.

I won't teach you how to use this site, as I assume you already know how to use the internet (at least I hope so).
However, here's how I wrote the walkthroughs:

  • Anything noted like this is part of the initial instructions from the challenge sites used.

  • Anything noted like this is code that is part of the instructions

  • 
#Anything noted like this is the solution code that I wrote

  • Anything noted like this is an explanation of my code

I won't necessarily write down the flags, as the goal is not the answer but the process.

The pages with 'like this' written in the table of contents are not yet accessible because I haven't finished them yet, don't worry, I'll do them one day.

If you're looking for something specific on a page, use 'ctrl + F' ( You probably already know this ).

Use the dark mode toggle in the top right corner to switch between light and dark mode. ( I made it especially for you! )

Use the 'Back to Top' button in the bottom right corner to quickly navigate back to the top of the page.
